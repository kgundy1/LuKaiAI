# Capture, Don't Guess — the LuKaiAI principle

> The single idea LuKaiAI is built around. Every surface — homepage, lessons, posts — derives from this. When in doubt, this file wins. Keep it current; if the meaning shifts, update here first, then everything else.

---

## The principle (one sentence)

**Capture, don't guess:** give AI the real specifics instead of letting it fill the gaps with the average.

## Why it matters (the problem it names)

Most people treat AI like a vending machine — type a vague request, expect the finished thing. But when you're vague, AI fills the gaps with the *average* of everything it has seen. Ask it to "build me a business" and you get the same generic model it hands everyone who asks that. That's not AI failing. That's AI **guessing**, because you didn't give it anything specific to work from.

The people who get *their* thing instead of the generic thing do one thing differently: they **capture** — they hand AI the real specifics (the actual screen, the real document, the exact situation, the precise constraint) instead of a vague approximation it has to guess at.

That's the whole game. Guessing gets you the average. Capturing gets you yours.

## The hook (public-facing)

> **AI gives everyone the same generic answer. Here's how to make it build yours.**

## How the principle shows up at three depths

1. **As the brand promise (homepage):** the generic-answer problem + "capture, don't guess" as the fix. This is what makes a stranger curious.
2. **As the method (throughout the lessons):** every time the workflow says "give Claude the real thing, not a description of it," that's capture, don't guess in action — describing your idea precisely, uploading real source documents instead of explaining them, scoping exactly what to build.
3. **As the most concrete move (the tactical version):** when you're stuck, confused, or staring at something you don't recognize — don't try to describe it in words, and don't go Googling. Screenshot what's on your screen, paste it into Claude, ask one short question. *Capturing the screen* is the simplest, most physical example of the whole principle. (This is the version currently taught in Module 1 — it should be framed as one example of the broader principle, not the whole of it.)

## How to say it (voice rules)

- **Show it, don't just state it.** The phrase only sticks through repetition and concrete examples. Use the generic-answer contrast (vague ask → average output) as the recurring proof.
- **Stay quiet-confident.** Not "this will change your life." The principle is interesting on its own; let it be.
- **Never use "course."** Say *method*, *workflow*, or *the way*. ("Capture, don't guess" is a method, not a course module.)
- **Them first, not you.** The principle is something *they* learn to do, not something you discovered. Founder story is one quiet line of proof, never the headline.
- **Don't overclaim.** "Free" always carries the honest tail: *you just need your own Claude subscription.* The principle makes AI work better; it doesn't guarantee any outcome.

## What NOT to do

- Don't lead with "bring your idea to life / turn ideas into reality" — it's the most saturated phrase in the category.
- Don't lead with a bare "AI isn't magic" — it's a think-piece cliché and a book title.
- Don't let "capture, don't guess" shrink back to *only* "screenshot when stuck." That's the example, not the meaning.
- Don't compete on "free / no code / ship real software" alone — direct competitors already own that ground. The principle is the differentiator.
